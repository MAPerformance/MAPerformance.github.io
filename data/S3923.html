Visitatio Sepulchri Dubliniensis

Date: 1300-1600

Visitatio Sepulchri

[…] Interim veniant ad ingressum chori Due Persone nude pedes, sub Personis Apostolorum Iohannis et Petri, indeute albis sine paruris cum tunicis, quarum Iohannes amictus tunica alba, palmam in manu gestans, Petrus vero rubea tunica indutus, claves in manu ferens ; et presicte Mulieres de Sepulcro revertentes et quasi de choro simul exeuntes dicat Maria per se Sequenciam : […]
Meanwhile let there come to the entrance of the choir two people with bare feet as if representing the apostles John and Peter clothed in white albs without ornamentation with surcouts, of whom John wears his white surcoat wrapped round him and carries a palm in his hand, but Peter wears a red surcoat and carries keys in his hands…
